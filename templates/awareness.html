🎣 Threat #1

Phishing Attacks

Fake messages designed to steal your credentials, money, and identity by impersonating banks, government bodies, and trusted brands.

80K+Phishing cases in India 2025
3.4BPhishing emails sent daily worldwide
36%Of breaches involve phishing
97%Users can't identify a phishing email

What is Phishing?

Phishing is when cybercriminals send fake emails, SMS (smishing), or WhatsApp messages pretending to be your bank, HDFC, SBI, IRCTC, or even UIDAI. They create a sense of urgency — "Your account will be blocked!" — to make you click a fake link and enter your credentials.

Once you enter your username, password, or OTP on their fake page, attackers get instant access to your real account and drain your money within minutes.

India saw a 175% rise in phishing attempts in 2023, with most targeting UPI and net banking users through fake government portals.

⚠️ Warning Signs to Watch For

📧
Suspicious sender address

Email from hdfc-support@gmail.com or sbi.secure@outlook.com — real banks never use free email providers.

🚨
Urgency & fear tactics

"Your account will be suspended in 24 hours" — designed to bypass your rational thinking.

🔗
Mismatched URLs

Hover over links — if it says hdfc.com but the URL shows hdfcbanck.xyz, it's fake.

🏛️
Fake government portals

Fake PM Kisan, Aadhaar update, or income tax refund pages that look exactly like the real site.

🎁
Too-good-to-be-true offers

"You've won ₹50 lakh in the KBC lottery" — a classic bait to collect your Aadhaar and bank details.

📎
Unexpected attachments

An "invoice" or "court notice" attachment you never expected — likely contains malware.

✅ What To Do
✅ Always verify sender email addresses carefully before clicking
✅ Go directly to the official website by typing the URL yourself
✅ Enable 2-factor authentication on all accounts
✅ Use CyberShield's URL scanner before visiting any suspicious link
✅ Report phishing to CERT-In at incidents@cert-in.org.in
❌ What NOT To Do
❌ Never click links in unsolicited emails or SMS
❌ Never enter your OTP, password, or PIN on a page you reached via a link
❌ Don't trust caller ID — numbers can be spoofed to look official
❌ Never download attachments from unknown senders
❌ Don't share Aadhaar or PAN details via email or WhatsApp

💀 Threat #2

Malware & Ransomware

Malicious software that hijacks your device, encrypts your files, and demands payment — often destroying everything even after you pay.

5thIndia is 5th most attacked country globally in 2023
165%Rise in ransomware attacks in India year-by-year
₹4 CrAverage ransom demand per attack
70%Attacks enter via email attachments

What is Malware?

Malware (malicious software) includes viruses, trojans, spyware, adware, and ransomware. It secretly installs on your device when you download cracked software, click fake ads, or open email attachments.

Ransomware is the most devastating type — it encrypts all your files and demands payment (usually in cryptocurrency) to restore access. AIIMS Delhi was crippled for days in 2022 after a ransomware attack.

Spyware silently records your keystrokes, capturing banking passwords and OTPs without you knowing anything is wrong.

⚠️ Warning Signs to Watch For

🐌
Sudden slowdowns

Your device becomes unusually slow — malware running in the background consumes resources.

🔒
Files become inaccessible

You try to open a file and get an error — ransomware may already be encrypting your data.

💸
Unexpected data charges

Spyware sends data to attackers, consuming mobile data in the background.

🖥️
Pop-ups and fake alerts

"Your PC is infected! Call this number" — fake antivirus alerts are themselves malware delivery tools.

🔋
Battery draining fast

On mobile, mining malware runs cryptocurrency mining on your phone using your battery and data.

📡
Unknown network activity

Network monitor shows traffic at odd hours when you're not using any apps.

✅ What To Do
✅ Keep OS and all apps updated — patches fix security holes malware exploits
✅ Install apps only from Google Play Store or Apple App Store
✅ Take regular backups to an offline drive or secure cloud storage
✅ Use reputable antivirus — Malwarebytes, Bitdefender, or Windows Defender
✅ Disconnect from internet immediately if you suspect ransomware
❌ What NOT To Do
❌ Never download cracked software, movies, or games from unofficial sites
❌ Don't enable "Install from unknown sources" unless you absolutely know what you're doing
❌ Never pay the ransom — there's no guarantee you'll get your files back
❌ Don't plug in random USB drives you find or receive as gifts
❌ Never click "Allow" on browser notification requests from unknown websites
🏥

Real Case: AIIMS Delhi Ransomware Attack — 2022

India's premier hospital had its entire network encrypted by ransomware. Patient records of millions were held hostage. The attackers demanded ₹200 crore in cryptocurrency. Hospital operations switched to manual paper-based systems for weeks, delaying critical patient care.

Lesson: Even large institutions fall victim. For individuals, regular offline backups are the only guaranteed protection against ransomware.

🎭 Threat #3

Social Engineering

Attackers manipulate human psychology rather than technology — exploiting trust, fear, and urgency to extract sensitive information.

95%Of all cyber breaches involve human manipulation
₹1 CrAverage loss per corporate social engineering attack
77%Targeted attacks use social engineering as entry point
6Psychological triggers attackers exploit

What is Social Engineering?

Social engineering exploits human psychology rather than system vulnerabilities. A caller pretends to be from your bank's fraud department, creating panic: "Your account is being misused, give me your OTP to block it." That OTP is exactly what gives them access.

Common forms include vishing (voice phishing calls), pretexting (inventing a believable scenario), baiting (leaving infected USB drives), and quid pro quo (offering something in exchange for information).

The six psychological triggers attackers exploit: Authority, Urgency, Social proof, Scarcity, Liking, and Reciprocity.

⚠️ Warning Signs to Watch For

📞
Unexpected calls from "officials"

CBI, police, TRAI, or bank officers calling out of nowhere demanding immediate action.

⏱️
Artificial urgency

"You have 30 minutes or we'll arrest you" — real authorities don't demand immediate action over phone.

🔐
Requests for OTP or password

No legitimate organization will ever ask for your OTP, CVV, or net banking password.

🤫
"Keep this confidential"

Scammers ask you to not tell family members — so no one can warn you it's a scam.

✅ What To Do
✅ Hang up and call back on the official number — verify the caller's identity
✅ Tell a trusted family member or friend before taking any action
✅ Remember: no real bank, police, or government body will call to ask for OTP
✅ Trust your gut — if something feels wrong, it probably is
❌ What NOT To Do
❌ Never share OTP, CVV, or passwords with anyone on phone
❌ Don't act immediately under pressure — take time to verify
❌ Don't trust caller ID — it can be spoofed to show bank numbers
❌ Never install remote access apps (AnyDesk, TeamViewer) when someone asks over phone
👮

Real Case: "Digital Arrest" Scam — Mumbai Professor Loses ₹3.8 Lakh

They said her Aadhaar was linked to a money laundering case and she was under "digital arrest" — she must stay on video call or face imprisonment. She sent ₹3.8 lakh in "bail money" over 4 days before her family intervened.

📱 Threat #4

UPI & OTP Fraud

Scammers exploit India's UPI revolution — tricking users into sharing OTPs, scanning QR codes, or approving payment requests that drain their accounts instantly.

₹1,087 CrLost to UPI fraud in India 2023
95K+UPI fraud complaints filed 2023
45 SecAverage time to drain an account after OTP theft
60%Victims are aged 18–35 years

How UPI Fraud Works

The "collect request" scam: A scammer sends you a UPI collect request saying "Please approve ₹1 to verify your account for the refund." Approving a collect request means MONEY GOES OUT from your account, not in.

The "QR code" scam: You're selling something on OLX. Buyer says "I'm sending money, scan this QR code to receive it." Scanning a payment QR code to receive money makes no sense — scanning sends money out.

OTP phishing: Caller says they're from your telecom company and need your OTP to "link your SIM." They use it to complete a UPI transaction.

⚠️ Red Flags in UPI Transactions

📲
Collect requests for "verification"

No legitimate refund, prize, or verification requires you to approve an incoming collect request.

📷
"Scan this to receive money"

You never need to scan a QR code to receive money. Scanning always means sending money out.

🔢
OTP requested to process refund

Refunds are processed automatically by banks. No OTP is needed from your side for a refund.

📞
Unknown caller asking your UPI PIN

Your UPI PIN is entered only by you in your own app. No bank or UPI company will ever ask for it.

✅ What To Do
✅ Always read the full UPI screen before entering your PIN — is money going OUT or IN?
✅ Block your UPI/bank immediately at 1800 (call your bank helpline) if fraud occurs
✅ Report to cybercrime.gov.in and call 1930 immediately after fraud
✅ Set daily UPI transaction limits in your banking app
❌ What NOT To Do
❌ Never enter your UPI PIN on a collect request or to "receive" money
❌ Never scan QR codes sent by strangers to "receive" payment
❌ Don't share your UPI PIN, OTP, or account number with anyone
❌ Never install apps a stranger asks you to install to "process your refund"
💸

Real Case: OLX Scam — Army Officer Loses ₹1.5 Lakh

An Army officer listed a sofa set on OLX. A buyer claiming to be a fellow soldier sent a collect request with a note "payment for sofa." The officer approved it thinking money was coming in. ₹1.5 lakh was debited instantly. The scammer had sent multiple collect requests and the officer approved all of them.

Lesson: Approving a UPI collect request always means money goes OUT. For selling, just share your UPI ID and ask the buyer to send — never approve collect requests from strangers.

🤖 Threat #5

AI Deepfake Scams

Artificial intelligence clones the face and voice of your relatives — creating real-time video calls that are indistinguishable from the real person.

230%Rise in deepfake scam cases in India
3 MinIs enough video footage to clone someone's face
96%Of deepfakes created target financial fraud
₹40L+Largest single deepfake fraud in India

How Deepfake Scams Work

Scammers harvest your relative's videos from social media — Instagram reels, Facebook posts, WhatsApp status updates. Using AI tools, they clone the face and voice in minutes.

They then video call you as your relative in an "emergency": "I had an accident, I'm in police custody, please transfer money immediately and don't tell anyone." The face and voice match perfectly.

Audio deepfakes (voice cloning) are even more common and just as dangerous — a single WhatsApp audio message is enough to clone someone's voice.

⚠️ How to Detect a Deepfake Call

👁️
Unnatural blinking or eye movement

Early deepfakes blink incorrectly or eyes don't track naturally. Look for it.

🗣️
Slightly off lip sync

The mouth movement may not perfectly match the words, especially on complex consonants.

🌟
Weird lighting or blurry edges

The face-swap boundary around hairline and neck often shows artifacts or inconsistent lighting.

❓
They can't answer personal questions

Ask something only your real relative would know — a childhood memory, a family in-joke.

✅ What To Do
✅ Set a family secret code word — agree on a word only family members know to verify identity
✅ Always hang up and call back on the known number before sending any money
✅ Make social media accounts private — limit video footage available to scammers
✅ Be suspicious of any emergency request involving money transfer, even if face matches
❌ What NOT To Do
❌ Don't trust a face on a video call alone — deepfakes are indistinguishable to most people
❌ Don't transfer money before calling them on their real number through your contacts
❌ Don't keep public video content of family members on social media without privacy settings
❌ Never trust "don't tell anyone" — that's always a manipulation tactic

Real Case: Kerala Businessman Loses ₹40 Lakh to AI Voice Clone

The businessman transferred the money before calling his manager on a different number — to find the real manager had no idea what happened.

📈 Threat #6

Fake Investment Scams

Fraudulent trading platforms and crypto apps that show you massive fake profits — then lock your funds and demand "taxes" before disappearing forever.

₹500 CrLost to fake investment apps in H1 2024
30%Monthly "guaranteed returns" promised by scammers
72%Victims recruited through WhatsApp groups
₹0Recovered by most victims after the scam

How Fake Investment Scams Work

You get added to a WhatsApp group by a stranger. A "mentor" (fake finance expert) shares daily stock tips. You see others in the group making money (they're all in on it). You invest a small amount — and the fake app shows you amazing profits.

Encouraged by fake profits, you invest more. When you try to withdraw, you're told you must pay "30% tax" or "verification fee" first. After you pay that, another fee appears. The cycle continues until you stop paying — then the app disappears.

These are called "pig butchering" scams — they fatten you up with fake gains before slaughtering your savings.

⚠️ Warning Signs to Watch For

💰
Guaranteed high returns

"Guaranteed 40% monthly returns" — legitimate investments never guarantee high returns. SEBI-registered advisors are prohibited from making such promises.

💬
WhatsApp/Telegram recruitment

Being added to investment groups by strangers is a classic entry point for these scams.

📱
Unknown apps not on Play Store

Fake trading apps are distributed via APK links outside the Play Store — a major red flag.

🔒
"Pay to withdraw" demands

Any real investment platform allows you to withdraw your money without upfront "tax" or "fee" payments.

✅ What To Do
✅ Verify broker/advisor registration at sebi.gov.in before investing
✅ Use only SEBI-registered platforms like Zerodha, Groww, Upstox
✅ Withdraw a small test amount first — if it's blocked, stop investing immediately
✅ Report to SEBI SCORES portal and cybercrime.gov.in
❌ What NOT To Do
❌ Never trust "guaranteed returns" — real investments carry real risk
❌ Don't install investment apps from links sent on WhatsApp or Telegram
❌ Never pay "tax" or "fee" to unlock your own investment profits
❌ Don't invest with strangers who contact you unsolicited
📉

Real Case: Bengaluru Software Engineer Loses ₹70 Lakh

He invested ₹70 lakh over 4 months — all showing amazing profits on the fake app. When he tried to withdraw, he was asked for ₹8 lakh in "income tax." The platform vanished the next day.

Lesson: This is a "pig butchering" scam. The romantic interest, the investment group, and the profits were all part of a coordinated script.

💼 Threat #7

Fake Job Scams

Fraudulent job offers from fake MNCs that steal your personal documents to open loans in your name — leaving you with debt you never took.

22K+Fake job scam victims in India 2023
₹8.5 CrLost to job fraud per month in 2023
68%Victims are students or recent graduates
₹2-5LAverage loan taken in victim's name

How Fake Job Scams Work

You apply for a job on a fake portal that mimics Indeed, Naukri, or LinkedIn. You get an instant "offer letter" via WhatsApp from a fake HR of Amazon, Google, or Infosys. The "work from home" salary is unbelievably high.

For "background verification" they ask for Aadhaar card, PAN card, bank statement, and a cancelled cheque. Once they have these documents, your identity is stolen — they take loans, open accounts, and commit fraud in your name.

Another variant asks you to pay "security deposit" or "training kit" fees before joining — and disappears after payment.

⚠️ Warning Signs of Fake Job Offers

💵
Unbelievably high salary

"₹1.5 lakh/month work from home for freshers" — real fresher salaries in India rarely exceed ₹40K.

📋
Documents demanded before joining

No legitimate company asks for Aadhaar, PAN, and bank details before an in-person interview and offer letter.

💳
Upfront payment required

"Pay ₹2000 for the laptop/training kit and we'll reimburse it" — legitimate companies never ask employees to pay upfront.

📧
Gmail/Yahoo email from "MNC"

Real companies like Amazon, Google, TCS communicate from their official @company.com email domains only.

✅ What To Do
✅ Verify the company exists at MCA21 portal (mca.gov.in) — check CIN number
✅ Call the company's official helpline (from their official website) to verify the job offer
✅ Check CIBIL report regularly — if loans appear you didn't take, report immediately
✅ Only apply through official websites or verified portals like Naukri, LinkedIn
❌ What NOT To Do
❌ Never share Aadhaar, PAN, or bank details before physically visiting an office
❌ Don't pay any upfront "deposit" or "kit fee" for a job
❌ Never respond to job offers received on WhatsApp from unknown numbers
❌ Don't trust flashy offer letters without meeting the employer in person

🪪 Threat #8

Identity Theft

Criminals steal your Aadhaar, PAN, and personal data to open bank accounts, take loans, and commit fraud — in your name, without you knowing.

1.4LIdentity fraud cases filed in India 2023
₹6.5LAverage financial damage per victim
18 MoAverage time victims take to discover theft
40%Cases involve Aadhaar-based identity fraud

How Identity Theft Happens

With just your Aadhaar number, PAN number, date of birth, and a photo — criminals can open bank mule accounts, apply for credit cards, and take personal loans. They combine data stolen from different data breaches to build a complete profile of you.

Data breaches are common — your phone number, email, and partial financial details may already be on the dark web from breaches of apps, hospitals, or government databases you never knew about.

Physical document theft is also common — discarded utility bills, old bank statements, or Aadhaar photocopies given at hotels or cybercafes can be used for identity fraud.

⚠️ Signs Your Identity May Be Stolen

💳
Unknown credit cards or loans on CIBIL

Check your free CIBIL report annually — unknown accounts are the clearest sign of identity theft.

📩
Loan collection calls you don't recognize

Calls about a loan you never took are a strong indicator someone has used your identity.

📬
Unfamiliar bank statements arriving

Bank documents or credit card bills for accounts you didn't open arriving at your address.

📵
SIM card suddenly stops working

SIM swapping — scammers transfer your mobile number to their SIM to receive OTPs for your accounts.

✅ What To Do
✅ Check your CIBIL score free at CIBIL.com or through apps like CRED once a year
✅ Lock your Aadhaar biometrics at myaadhaar.uidai.gov.in when not in use
✅ Shred or burn documents with personal info before discarding
✅ File FIR immediately and report to cybercrime.gov.in if identity theft occurs
✅ Use masked Aadhaar (shows only last 4 digits) for non-critical verification
❌ What NOT To Do
❌ Never post photos of your Aadhaar, PAN, or passport on any platform including WhatsApp
❌ Don't give original document photocopies without writing "For [purpose] only, date" on them
❌ Never leave sensitive documents in cars, at hotels, or at cybercafes
❌ Don't link all financial accounts to one email — if that's compromised, everything is
❌ Don't use public Wi-Fi for banking or sharing personal details
